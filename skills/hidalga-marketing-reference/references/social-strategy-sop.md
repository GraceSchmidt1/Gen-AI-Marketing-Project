# Social Strategy SOP

*Source: Q1_SOP_and_review_cadence.docx — Hidalga Technologies*

---

## Objective
Produce measurable learning, improve engagement with the right audience, and generate at least one qualified conversation or lead each month that can be directly traced back to social media content. Content must stay aligned with decision-makers in oncology clinics, improve over time through structured review, and contribute meaningfully to pipeline development.

---

## Target Audience

**Primary:** COOs, CFOs, practice administrators, and RCM leaders at small-to-mid-sized community oncology practices. Directly impacted by prior authorization delays, denial-related revenue loss, administrative burden, and lack of visibility into authorization status.

**Secondary (use selectively — do not generalize):** Oncologists, nurse navigators, prior authorization specialists, MSO leaders. Each post should prioritize a single primary audience.

---

## Posting Cadence

- **LinkedIn:** 2–3 times per week (primary channel)
- **Instagram & Facebook:** Same frequency as LinkedIn (secondary channels)
- Content stays consistent in theme across channels but is tailored for each platform per platform-specific guidelines
- Maintain a rolling pipeline with at least 2–3 weeks of content planned and approved at all times
- All content tracked and managed through Microsoft Planner and/or Loomly

---

## Content Direction Rules

- All content falls within one of the five content pillars: Thought Leadership, Product Development, Events & Partnerships, Patient-Centered Storytelling, or Team Spotlights
- **Lead with a specific problem**, not a general idea — posts that introduce a clear issue early and explain its operational impact consistently outperform abstract content
- Industry commentary must connect back to clinic workflows or financial outcomes
- Team updates must be framed to reinforce expertise, product development, or mission alignment

---

## Video Content Requirements

- Video is a **required** component of the content strategy, not optional
- Minimum one video every two weeks, prioritized for LinkedIn first, then adapted for other platforms
- Formats: 20–40 second explanations of prior auth challenges, breakdowns of industry updates, walkthroughs of workflow inefficiencies or product functionality
- Strong video structure: clear relevant problem → concise takeaway → no unnecessary complexity

---

## Monthly Performance Review (Required)

Document in OneNote (or approved location) and link within Planner. Must include:

1. **Metrics summary:** Posting volume, engagement, clicks, conversations generated
2. **What changed:** Shifts in audience focus, messaging style, content format, or CTAs
3. **What worked:** Specific post examples with reasoning (specificity + financial/operational pain points = performance)
4. **What didn't work:** General content, missing problem statements, non-audience-specific openers, culture posts not tied to expertise
5. **At least one experiment:** Test different messaging, format, or specificity level — document outcome even if inconclusive

---

## Lead & Conversation Tracking (Required)

At least one meaningful interaction per month must be documented and tied back to specific content. Record:
- Source post
- Audience type
- Specific message or idea that triggered engagement

---

## Success Criteria

Strategy is working when:
- Content becomes more targeted over time
- Engagement becomes more consistent
- Clear patterns emerge around what resonates with intended audiences
- Improvement is visible in **click-through behavior**, not just impressions or reactions
- Real conversations can be traced back to specific content
